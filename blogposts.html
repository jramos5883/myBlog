Preperation and Research

2/18/2023

I've spent the past weeks preparing to apply for tech positions. I've created my Linked'in account, rewritten my resume, created a portfolio webpage, and got some real world coding experience by building a website for a resturant. Honestly, from looking back at where I started my coding journey to where I am now, I am happy with all that I have learned and accomplished thus far. It feels as if I am moving into a new phase of my coding journey. From my studies and experiences over the past few months, I feel ready to put myself out there and show the tech world what I'm made of. And now that I've completed my initial preperations to start applying, it is time to do...

...more research! Woo hoo! Trust me, I am excited about it! As a self taught developer, research has been a huge part of my process. Over the past few months, I've come to realize that becoming a good programmer means becoming a good problem solver. Especially being a self taught developer, learning to utilize sites like stack overflow and google to efficently find solutions for bugs or other problems is a skill in itself. As I continue on my coding journey, I am happy with the foundation of knowledge that I have accquired up until this point. While I apply for coding positions, I plan on building more personal projects, practicing more leet code questions, and learning more relavant technologies. From my research, I have decided that the next technology worth learning for myself and my future career would be React. My last note, as I research the best ways to teach myself React, is that I've come to learn to enjoy the process of coding. There is a cycle of preperation and research that is needed to learn efficiently and stay motivated. As I continue to imporve on my coding abilities, I also want to continue to improve on my so called "process".

Show Your Work!

2/15/2023

After reading the book, "Show Your Work! written by Austin Kleon, I've decided to create a website/blog where I can share some of the experiences and knowledge that I've acquired during my slef taught coding journey. I want to use this blog as a way to share with the world what I'm learning, what I'm working on, and what I want to accomplish in the future.

Now that I've spent the past few months teaching myself the basics of coding and web development, I am eager to make my break into the tech industry. My short term goals are to continue learning everything that I can about coding and land my first job as a junior software engineer. Some long term goals that I have are building a brand for myself, building a email newsletter, and building a community.
